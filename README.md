# Neurodivergent Minor Therapeutic Integrity and Anti-Coercion Act

## What is this?
A model state legislation artifact designed to protect neurodivergent minors from coercive behavioral suppression, forced compliance production, communication-blocking, regulation-blocking, deprivation-based, retaliatory, leverage-backed, performance-conditioning, and materially deceptive treatment practices in covered settings.

This is a practical public-use artifact. It is meant to be read, shared, adapted, printed, and taken to legislators, advocates, counsel, journalists, institutions, and organizations.

## Why should I care?
This Act addresses a specific gap.

It protects neurodivergent minors while preserving supportive, developmentally appropriate services that help a minor communicate, regulate, learn, adapt, and remain safe without independently prohibited conduct. It is written to regulate observable misconduct and accountability failures rather than viewpoint alone.

## What is the claim?
The claim is practical and structural:

a state can protect neurodivergent minors from coercive compliance production and deprivation-based control without imposing viewpoint-only liability, by regulating independently provable conduct, communication blocking, regulation blocking, materially deceptive claims, records abuse, retaliation, leverage, and related accountability failures.

## What is this not?
This artifact is not:
- a ban on ordinary family or religious conversation outside covered settings
- a viewpoint-only speech restriction
- a prohibition on supportive, developmentally appropriate services
- enacted law in any jurisdiction

It is model legislation for adaptation and use.
